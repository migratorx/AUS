Technology Explained

NFC Tags (Near Field Communication)

NFC tags are small, unpowered chips that are placed at specific checkpoints. A guard's patrol device must be brought very close (within a few inches) to the tag to scan it. This action provides definitive, timestamped proof that the guard was physically present at that exact location. It's ideal for verifying checks of specific doors, panels, or rooms.

Beacons

Beacons are small, battery-powered devices that continuously broadcast a low-energy Bluetooth signal. A patrol device automatically detects this signal when it enters the beacon's range, which can cover a larger area like an entire lobby, platform, or hallway. This confirms a guard's presence within a general zone without requiring a specific action, making it perfect for monitoring larger, open spaces.
